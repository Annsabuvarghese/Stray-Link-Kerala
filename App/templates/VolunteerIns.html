Welcome to StrayLink Volunteer Program 🐾

Thank you for joining our mission to rescue and care for stray animals.

Welcome New Member

We are thrilled to have you as part of the StrayLink volunteer community. Our platform connects caring individuals who want to help stray animals find safety, medical care, and loving homes.

As a volunteer, you will help respond to rescue reports, verify animal conditions, assist with adoption processes, and guide the community toward responsible animal care.

Every small action you take can save a life. Together we can build a safer world for stray animals.

How Rescue Works

When someone reports an injured or distressed stray animal on StrayLink, the report becomes visible to volunteers in nearby locations.

As a volunteer, your responsibility is to:

  • Review the reported animal details.
  • Check the location and urgency.
  • Visit the location if possible.
  • Confirm whether the animal has been rescued.
  • Update the rescue status in the system.

If the animal requires medical care, contact nearby veterinarians or rescue groups for assistance.

Volunteer Duties

Your responsibilities include:

  • Responding to rescue reports in your area.
  • Helping injured or abandoned animals.
  • Verifying rescue and adoption reports.
  • Guiding adopters about pet care.
  • Supporting responsible animal welfare.

Remember, volunteers act as the bridge between the community, rescuers, and adopters.

Important Things to Remember

  • Always prioritize your personal safety.
  • Do not handle aggressive animals without help.
  • Contact professional rescuers when needed.
  • Be respectful and kind to everyone in the community.
  • Only mark animals as rescued if verified.

Accurate information and responsible action are critical for ensuring animal safety.

Be Like a Family

StrayLink is not just a platform — it is a community of compassionate people who believe every animal deserves love and care.

Treat fellow volunteers, adopters, and reporters with kindness and respect. Collaboration makes rescue efforts faster and more effective.

Together we can create a network that ensures no stray animal is left helpless.
